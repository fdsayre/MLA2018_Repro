TAKE-AWAYS

Why Academic Librarians and Reproducibility?

While talking with faculty I've needed to be proactive about addressing what is an obvious and I think reasonable question many of them have: Why me? Why is a librarian talking to them about reproducibility? We’re not researchers. Or, actually, we are, but our research tends to be about how people use and organize information. I’m totally comfortable talking about methods and reproducibility of systematic reviews, or bibliometric research, but when I think about research I tend to think about it in terms of how it is structured, organized, made available, and a little about how it is evaluated. I tend to think about research in terms of how it is reported or packaged.

Guidelines

Okay, So: Reproducibility Guidelines, much like treatment guidelines, are generally consensus statements of best practices towards the goal of improving the reproducibility of results. Again, we really like centering our discussion on guidelines because they (a) come from researchers and publishers themselves, and (b) demonstrate some of the commonalities and differences between disciplines and organizations. Just want to mention that we are only looking at 3 guidelines here (TOP, ASA, NIH). There are MANY more guidelines, incuding from funders, journals, and for specific disciplines, methodologies, research areas, and even conferences. Amy and I are currently working on a research project that tries to make these easier to find and use, so if you’re interested in guideliens for your specific area please get in touch with us
So, first I’m going to talk about the Transparency and Openness Promotion (TOP) Guidelines. The TOP guidelines were developed by a group of scientists, journal editors, and open science advocates and are meant to be adopted by journals. They are a flexible set of 9 criteria that can adopted at various levels of enforcement in order to meet disciplinary norms and goals. As of October of last year Over 5,000* journals and organizations have become signatories to the TOP Guidelines, including all Elsevier journals. While they are meant to be adopted by journals, once they are fully implemented they have significant affects of researchers.
